# 公开信：呼吁前沿 AI 提供商在中低收入国家实施分级定价

# Open Letter to Frontier AI Providers: Adopt Tiered Pricing in Lower- and Middle-Income Countries

*Draft v0.1 · 草案 · 2026-05-13*

**致 / Addressed to**：OpenAI、Anthropic、Google DeepMind、xAI、Meta AI、Mistral、Cohere，以及其他前沿 AI 模型与服务提供商。
OpenAI, Anthropic, Google DeepMind, xAI, Meta AI, Mistral, Cohere, and other frontier AI model and service providers.

**抄送 / Cc**：World Bank, UNESCO, ITU, regional digital ministries.

---

## 中文

### 一、我们的诉求

我们呼吁所有前沿 AI 提供商，在 **2026 年内** 公开承诺并实施一项基于购买力平价（PPP）的分级定价方案，让中低收入国家的普通用户能以本地可负担的价格使用先进 AI 服务。

具体地，我们请求：

1. **PPP 定价**：在 GNI 人均低于 USD 4,500（世界银行中低收入国家阈值）的国家与地区，前沿订阅价格不应高于该国 GNI 人均的 **0.5% / 月**。
2. **公开透明**：在官方价格页面以独立栏目列出各国差异化价格，而不是依赖账号注册地的暗箱判定。
3. **学生与研究者通道**：为已认证的高校学生、独立研究者、公立学校教师提供 **免费或近免费** 的前沿模型访问额度。
4. **支付包容性**：在合规允许的范围内，接受当地主流支付方式（UPI、M-Pesa、SPEI、PIX、微信/支付宝、Mada 等），而不是只接受美元信用卡。
5. **地域非歧视**：在没有明确法律禁止的地区，不应仅以"商业便利"为由拒绝服务；对暂时无法提供服务的地区，公开列出原因与解除时间表。

### 二、为什么是现在

- **价差悬殊**。一份每月 USD 20 的前沿订阅，在美国约占中位月收入的 0.4%，在印度约占 7–10%，在尼日利亚则可超过 15%。同等服务的负担差距高达 30 倍。
- **能力代差正在固化**。前沿模型与上一代模型在编程、研究、推理上的差距，已从"略好一点"扩大到"另一档"。被价格挡在前沿之外的人，正在被结构性地排除在新型生产力之外。
- **先例俯拾即是**。Netflix、Spotify、YouTube Premium、Apple App Store、Microsoft 365、Adobe Creative Cloud 早已实施分级定价；制药业通过 PPP 定价让 HIV 抗病毒治疗从每年 USD 10,000 降至 USD 75。这不是激进要求，而是数字时代的成熟实践。
- **你们自己的使命宣言要求这件事**。多家前沿实验室将"为全人类受益"写入愿景。当一项工具被定义为"为全人类"，却让占世界人口 60% 以上的人付不起，使命宣言就需要兑现，而不是修辞。

### 三、我们承认的现实

- 前沿训练成本高昂，公司需要盈利以持续投资。
- 部分地区因出口管制、合规、支付基础设施而存在客观限制。
- 反套利（VPN 绕区）机制需要工程投入。

这些都是真实的约束。但**没有一项约束能解释当前定价结构在中低收入国家造成的"事实上的禁入"**。其他行业已经证明：在保留主要市场利润的同时实施 PPP 定价，是可行的、可盈利的、并能扩大长期市场的。

### 四、我们将做的事

作为公民社会一方，AIpower 承诺：

- 每季度发布 **《全球 AI 访问门槛地图》**，公开各前沿服务在各国的可访问性与实际价格负担；
- 配合愿意试点 PPP 定价的提供商，提供试点地区的反馈、滥用监测、用户教育；
- 不对采取行动的提供商进行抵制性指责，对持续无视者将通过年度《AI 垄断风险报告》系统性记录。

### 五、邀请联署

如果你认同上述诉求——无论你是个人用户、研究者、从业者、行业内部员工、其他公民组织、政府或国际机构代表——请通过 PR 把你的名字或机构加入 [signatories.md](signatories.md)。

我们将在收集到 **首批 500 个联署** 后，正式将本信送达各受信方，并向公众发布。

## English

### 1. What we ask

We call on every frontier AI provider to **publicly commit, within 2026**, to a purchasing-power-parity (PPP) tiered pricing program so that ordinary users in lower- and middle-income countries can access advanced AI at locally affordable prices.

Specifically:

1. **PPP pricing.** In countries with a GNI per capita below USD 4,500 (the World Bank lower-middle-income threshold), frontier-tier subscriptions should not exceed **0.5% of GNI per capita per month**.
2. **Transparency.** Publish a clear, country-by-country pricing page rather than hiding regional differences behind opaque account-registration logic.
3. **Students & researchers.** Offer **free or near-free** access to verified university students, independent researchers, and public-school teachers.
4. **Payment inclusion.** Where legally permitted, accept locally dominant payment methods (UPI, M-Pesa, SPEI, PIX, WeChat/Alipay, Mada, and others) — not just USD credit cards.
5. **Non-discrimination by geography.** Do not refuse service in a region purely for commercial convenience absent a clear legal prohibition. Where service is temporarily unavailable, publish the reason and the timeline for resolution.

### 2. Why now

- **The price gap is enormous.** A USD 20/month frontier subscription is roughly 0.4% of median monthly income in the United States, 7–10% in India, and over 15% in Nigeria. The burden gap reaches 30×.
- **The capability gap is widening.** The performance distance between frontier and previous-generation models in coding, research, and reasoning has grown from "slightly better" to "a different tier." People priced out of the frontier are being structurally excluded from a new kind of productivity.
- **Precedents are everywhere.** Netflix, Spotify, YouTube Premium, the Apple App Store, Microsoft 365, and Adobe Creative Cloud have long implemented tiered pricing. Pharma's PPP pricing brought HIV antiretroviral therapy from USD 10,000/year to USD 75/year. What we ask is not radical; it is mature practice from the digital era.
- **Your own mission statements demand it.** Multiple frontier labs have written *"for the benefit of all humanity"* into their founding documents. When a tool is defined as "for all humanity" but priced beyond reach for over 60% of the world's population, the mission needs to be honored — not just recited.

### 3. What we acknowledge

- Frontier training is genuinely expensive, and companies need profits to sustain investment.
- Some regions face real constraints from export controls, compliance, and payment infrastructure.
- Anti-arbitrage (VPN-region-hopping) is a non-trivial engineering investment.

These are real. **But none of them explains the de-facto exclusion the current pricing structure produces in lower- and middle-income countries.** Other industries have shown that PPP pricing is technically feasible, commercially profitable, and expands long-term markets while preserving margin in primary markets.

### 4. What we will do

As civil society, AIpower commits to:

- Publish a quarterly **Global AI Access Barrier Map**, documenting accessibility and effective price burden of frontier services country by country;
- Support providers willing to pilot PPP pricing with feedback, abuse monitoring, and user education in pilot regions;
- Refrain from boycott-style campaigns against providers that act, while continuing to systematically document persistent non-action in our annual *AI Monopoly Risk Report*.

### 5. Invitation to co-sign

If you agree with the asks above — whether you are an individual user, a researcher, a practitioner, an employee inside one of the addressed labs, another civil-society organization, a government, or an international body — please add your name or institution to [signatories.md](signatories.md) via a Pull Request.

We will formally deliver this letter to the addressees and release it publicly once the first **500 co-signatures** have been gathered.
