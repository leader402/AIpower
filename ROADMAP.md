# 路线图 / Roadmap

> 路线图是一份滚动文件。每一项都要可验证、可问责。
> A rolling document. Every item must be verifiable and accountable.

---

## 中文

### 阶段 0 · 奠基（0–3 个月）

- [ ] 仓库上线，发布双语章程、宣言、行为准则。
- [ ] 招募首批 30 名正式成员，覆盖至少 5 个国家/地区。
- [ ] 组建 5 个支柱工作组的临时召集人。
- [ ] 选举首届指导委员会（5–7 人）。
- [ ] 公开通讯渠道：邮件列表 + Matrix/Discord/Telegram 镜像。

### 阶段 1 · 第一次发声（3–6 个月）

- [ ] 发布《AI 平权倡议·第 1 期月报》。
- [ ] 完成"全球 AI 访问门槛地图 v0.1"：哪些国家、哪些支付方式、哪些 IP 无法访问哪些主流模型。
- [ ] 发起首封公开信，议题候选：
  - 反对以"安全"为名提高开源模型的合规门槛；
  - 呼吁主流 AI 服务为发展中国家提供分级定价；
  - 呼吁前沿实验室公开关键能力测评的细则。
- [ ] 启动 *Fund-a-Mile* 众筹：每项资助一名因价格无法使用前沿 AI 的学生 / 研究者 / 教师 6 个月订阅。

### 阶段 2 · 建设公共物（6–12 个月）

- [ ] 发布首份《AI 垄断风险年度报告》。
- [ ] 与至少 3 个开源模型项目建立"镜像保护"协议（断链备份、社区接管预案）。
- [ ] 发布"低价 AI 接入指南"开源工具，帮助普通用户找到最便宜的合规通道。
- [ ] 用 5 种以上语言出版 *AI 基本素养* 入门读本（CC-BY-SA）。

### 阶段 3 · 制度化（12 个月以后）

- [ ] 在至少 1 个国家/地区注册为非营利法律实体（以便接受机构捐赠、签订合作协议）。
- [ ] 与高校、图书馆、社区组织合作，开设线下 AI 普及点。
- [ ] 推动至少 1 项立法/标准提案被官方采纳或正式回应。

### Fund-a-Mile 资助清单（示例）

| 项目 | 单笔预算 | 受益人 |
|---|---|---|
| 1 名学生 6 个月前沿模型订阅 | USD 120 | 发展中国家学生 |
| 1 个母语 AI 教程视频系列 | USD 500 | 非英语社区 |
| 1 期独立监测报告 | USD 1500 | 全体公众 |
| 1 项开源镜像保护 | USD 300 | 全体开发者 |

资助清单滚动维护，每季度更新。所有支出公开在 `finance/`。

## English

### Phase 0 · Foundation (0–3 months)

- [ ] Launch the repository with bilingual charter, manifesto, and code of conduct.
- [ ] Onboard the first 30 Members across at least 5 countries/regions.
- [ ] Appoint interim coordinators for the 5 working groups.
- [ ] Elect the first Council (5–7 members).
- [ ] Open public channels: a mailing list plus Matrix/Discord/Telegram mirrors.

### Phase 1 · First voice (3–6 months)

- [ ] Publish *AIpower Monthly · Issue 1*.
- [ ] Release the *Global AI Access Barrier Map v0.1*: which countries, payment methods, and IPs are blocked from which major models.
- [ ] Issue the first open letter. Candidate topics:
  - oppose "safety"-framed regulation that raises compliance cost for open-source models;
  - call for tiered pricing of mainstream AI services in developing countries;
  - call on frontier labs to publish detailed methodology of key capability evaluations.
- [ ] Launch *Fund-a-Mile*: crowdfund 6-month frontier-model subscriptions for students / researchers / teachers priced out of access.

### Phase 2 · Build public goods (6–12 months)

- [ ] Publish the first *Annual AI Monopoly Risk Report*.
- [ ] Sign "mirror preservation" agreements with at least 3 open-model projects (cold-backup + community-takeover plan).
- [ ] Release an open-source *Low-Cost AI Access Guide* tool helping ordinary users find the cheapest compliant path.
- [ ] Publish an *AI Literacy Primer* in 5+ languages under CC-BY-SA.

### Phase 3 · Institutionalize (12+ months)

- [ ] Incorporate as a non-profit legal entity in at least one country (to accept institutional grants and sign formal partnerships).
- [ ] Partner with universities, libraries, and community organizations to host offline AI-literacy hubs.
- [ ] Get at least one legislative or standards proposal officially adopted or formally responded to.

### Fund-a-Mile (examples)

| Item | Budget | Beneficiary |
|---|---|---|
| 6-month frontier subscription for 1 student | USD 120 | Student in developing country |
| 1 native-language AI tutorial series | USD 500 | Non-English community |
| 1 independent monitoring report | USD 1,500 | General public |
| 1 open-model mirror preservation | USD 300 | All developers |

The list rolls forward and is updated quarterly. All expenditures are published in `finance/`.
